Statement of the Problem

Dengue remains a major public health concern in the Philippines, particularly among children who are often exposed to mosquito breeding sites in school environments. The increasing population of Aedes aegypti mosquitoes, combined with inadequate sanitation and the continued use of chemical-based larvicides, poses risks to human health and the environment. Despite the known medicinal properties of Annona muricata (guyabano), limited studies focus on the antimicrobial and larvicidal potential of its seeds for community-based mosquito control.

Specifically, this study seeks to answer the following questions:

  1. What is the effect of Annona muricata seed extract on the mortality of Aedes aegypti mosquito larvae?
  2. Is there a significant difference in larval mortality when exposed to varying concentrations of the seed extract?
  3. Which concentration of Annona muricata seed extract is most effective in eliminating mosquito larvae?
  4. Can Annona muricata seed extract be considered a safe and eco-friendly alternative to chemical larvicides used in schools?
