Statement of the Problem

Plastic waste has become a serious environmental problem in urban areas such as Marikina City, contributing to pollution, flooding, and marine ecosystem degradation. Conventional plastics are non-biodegradable and pose long-term risks to the environment. This situation highlights the need for sustainable and locally sourced alternatives.

Cassava starch (Manihot esculenta) is an abundant and renewable agricultural resource, while chitosan derived from chitin found in marine waste from Manila Bay offers potential as a natural biopolymer. However, limited local studies have explored the combined use of cassava starch and chitosan for bioplastic production.

This study aims to determine the feasibility of developing a localized bioplastic using cassava starch and Manila Bay chitosan.

  1. Can cassava starch be effectively used as a base material for bioplastic production?
  2. Does the addition of chitosan improve the strength and flexibility of cassava-based bioplastic?
  3. Is the developed bioplastic a viable and eco-friendly alternative to conventional plastic materials?
