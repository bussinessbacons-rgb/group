Statement of the Problem

The increasing dependence on chemical fertilizers has contributed to environmental degradation and the growing phosphorus crisis, which threatens food security and water quality. To avoid repeating this crisis, there is a need to explore sustainable and organic fertilizer alternatives.

Organic bone meal, which contains phosphorus and calcium, may offer a potential substitute for conventional fertilizers such as Ammonium Phosphate. However, limited studies compare their effectiveness on Yardlong Bean (Vigna unguiculata ssp. sesquipedalis) growth.

This study aims to determine the effectiveness of organic bone meal compared to Ammonium Phosphate as fertilizer for Yardlong Bean plants.

  1. What are the effects of bone meal fertilizer on Yardlong Bean plants in terms of:
    • Number of leaves
    • Height (average in inches)
  2. What are the effects of Ammonium Phosphate on Yardlong Bean plants in terms of:
    • Number of leaves
    • Height (average in inches)
  3. Is there a significant difference between plants treated with bone meal and those treated with Ammonium Phosphate?
