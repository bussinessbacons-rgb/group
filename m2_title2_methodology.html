Methodology

This study employed an experimental research design to compare the effects of organic bone meal and Ammonium Phosphate on Yardlong Bean plant growth. Prior to fertilizer application, soil pH levels were tested, soil quantities were measured, and seeds were planted 2 cm below the soil surface.

The plants were allowed to germinate before fertilizer application to avoid reduced germination rates. Two experimental setups were prepared: Set A used liquid bone meal fertilizer, while Set B used Ammonium Phosphate. Six plants were grown in each setup using recycled containers.

Plant height and number of leaves were recorded weekly for four weeks. Fertilizers were applied every fourteen (14) days, and plants were watered regularly. Data gathered were subjected to statistical analysis using an independent t-test.
