Results

The results showed noticeable differences in plant growth between Yardlong Bean plants treated with organic bone meal and those treated with Ammonium Phosphate. Plants treated with Ammonium Phosphate generally exhibited a higher average number of leaves and greater maximum height.

Statistical analysis using an independent t-test revealed a significant difference in the number of leaves between the two treatments, leading to the rejection of the null hypothesis for leaf count. However, no significant difference was found in plant height, resulting in the acceptance of the null hypothesis for height.

These findings suggest that while Ammonium Phosphate promotes faster leaf growth, organic bone meal supports steady and continuous plant development.
